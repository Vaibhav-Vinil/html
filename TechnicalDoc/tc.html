Introduction

Plants are essential to life on Earth, providing oxygen, food, and habitat for many organisms.

They play a crucial role in the ecosystem, helping to regulate the atmosphere and providing resources for human use.

Chapter One

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy.

This process occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells, where chlorophyll absorbs light.

Photosynthesis: 6CO2 + 6H2O + light → C6H12O6 + 6O2

    Plant respiration is the process of breaking down glucose to release energy for cellular activities.

    This occurs in the mitochondria of plant cells and is essential for growth and development.

    Respiration: C6H12O6 + 6O2 → 6CO2 + 6H2O + energy
